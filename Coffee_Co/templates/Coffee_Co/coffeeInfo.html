Long Black

A Long Black is made by pouring a double shot of espresso over hot water. It’s strong and smooth, with a rich crema on top. Popular in Australia and New Zealand.
Ratio: 1 part espresso (double shot) to 2 parts hot water (approx. 1:2).

Flat White

A Flat White is an espresso-based drink with steamed milk and a thin layer of velvety microfoam. It has a strong coffee flavor and creamy texture, perfect for those who want a balanced taste.
Ratio: 1 part espresso (double shot) to 3 parts steamed milk with microfoam (approx. 1:3).

Cappuccino

A Cappuccino combines equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. It has a bold coffee taste with a thick frothy top, often dusted with cocoa or cinnamon.
Ratio: 1 part espresso to 1 part steamed milk to 1 part milk foam (1:1:1).

Latte

A Latte contains espresso with a larger amount of steamed milk and a small layer of foam. It’s creamier and milder than a cappuccino, ideal for those who prefer a smooth, milky coffee.
Ratio: 1 part espresso to 5 parts steamed milk with a small foam layer (approx. 1:5).
